01 / Research

Planetary magmatic systems

Experiments and models that connect mineral-scale records to planetary-scale evolution.

Current research

Experimental petrology of planetary magmas

A

Moon

Lunar magmatism & redox evolution

My current work investigates the redox state and differentiation of lunar magmas using high-temperature experiments, mineral–melt geochemistry, and trace-element partitioning. I focus particularly on olivine–melt vanadium partitioning as an oxybarometer, with applications to Apollo, Chang’e-5, and lunar meteorite materials.

B

Mars

Martian magma differentiation

I am interested in how Martian mantle-derived melts differentiate to form evolved lithologies. Experimental petrology, fractional crystallization, volatile-bearing systems, and thermodynamic modelling—including MAGEMin—provide complementary ways to test the conditions and pathways of Martian magmatic evolution.

C

Mercury · Emerging interest

Magmatism under highly reduced conditions

As a future research direction, I am interested in applying experimental and geochemical approaches to the highly reduced magmatic systems of Mercury. These environments offer a valuable test of how extreme redox conditions influence phase relations, element partitioning, and crust-forming processes.

Previous research

Granitic pegmatites & rare-metal mineralization

My previous work examined the petrogenesis and mineralization of granite–pegmatite systems, including W–Sn and Li–Nb–Ta deposits. I investigated how volatile exsolution, boundary-layer melts, and tourmaline records illuminate rare-metal enrichment during late-stage magmatic evolution.
