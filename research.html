Research

Understanding magmas across planetary bodies

I combine controlled experiments, microanalysis, and thermodynamic modelling to reconstruct how magmas form, evolve, and record their physical and chemical environments.

01

Lunar geochemistry

Lunar magmatism and redox evolution

My work investigates the redox state and differentiation of lunar magmas using experimental calibration and mineral–melt geochemistry. A particular focus is trace-element partitioning between olivine and melt—including vanadium—as a quantitative record of oxygen fugacity in lunar systems.

02

Methods

Experimental petrology of planetary magmas

I conduct high-temperature experiments under controlled pressure, temperature, and redox conditions to simulate planetary magmatic processes. These include 1-atm gas-mixing furnace experiments and piston-cylinder experiments, paired with detailed analysis of experimental run products.

03

Silicic systems

Lunar silicic magmas and granite formation

I study possible origins of lunar silicic melts and granitic compositions. This work evaluates fractional crystallization, KREEP-related processes, crustal assimilation or interaction, and silicate liquid immiscibility as mechanisms for producing evolved lunar lithologies.

04

Terrestrial analogues

Volatile-rich magmatic systems and pegmatites

I am also interested in how volatile exsolution and boundary-layer melts redistribute elements during late-stage magmatic evolution. Tourmaline records and lithium enrichment in pegmatitic systems provide complementary evidence for these processes.
